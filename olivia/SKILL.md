**Obs Day 2 patterns (Indian Trail Jr. High + Army Trail Elementary, Mar 19):**
1. Proactive room presence — moving through the room intentionally, positioning where support is needed before being asked
2. Bilingual instructional support — using Spanish strategically to scaffold ELL instruction, not just translating
3. Strong student relationships — trust built, students respond more readily, accept redirection
4. Nonverbal behavior management — proximity, eye contact, gesture instead of verbal correction

**The Moves That Matter framework:**
- Move #1: Ask Don't Tell — questions that prompt student thinking, build independence
- Move #2: Feedback That Grows — Notice → Name → Next Step (3-part formula)
- Five practice games: Tell or Ask?, Question Knockout, Feedback Level Up, Feedback Makeover, Feedback Madlibs
- Free game tools: teachersdeserveit.com/paragametools

**Resource standards for all ASD4 materials:**
- PDF format, one page preferred
- Generous margins (62pt+), 16pt between checklist items
- Black and white primary, teal accents only
- Branding in footer only — clean and breathable
- Include SAY THIS scripts and LOOK FOR success indicators
- Written for paraprofessionals — warm, direct, no jargon, validates their work
- Strategy-based — teacher walks away with something to use Monday morning
