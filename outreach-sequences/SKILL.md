# TDI-Outreach-Sequences-Skill
**Lead: Jim Ford — Reviewed by Kristin Williams — Last updated March 30, 2026**

---

## Purpose

This skill document defines Jim Ford's outreach process. Jim is a setter. His job is one thing: book a meeting between a district decision-maker and Rae Hughart. Every task, every call, every email in this document serves that single outcome.

---

## Jim's role — stated precisely

Jim is not a closer. He does not pitch TDI's full value proposition. He does not negotiate. He does not make commitments. His finish line is a calendar invite with Rae's name on it.

Everything Jim does is in service of that one outcome.

---

## The lead list

Jim works from the TDI District Lead List v3 — 44 districts across IL, IN, and WI, structured with contact names, entry angles, cluster logic, and priority levels. The list is organized around paraprofessional and SpEd support as the primary entry angle across all contacts.

Priority levels:
- Warm — named contact, specific entry angle, verified or near-verified contact info
- Cold — title only or needs contact verification before calling

---

## The sequence structure

**Warm leads:**
- Touch 1: Call on day of assignment
- Touch 2: Email follow-up same day if no answer, or next day
- Touch 3: Follow-up email 3 days after Touch 2
- Touch 4: Final touch 5 days after Touch 3
- Archive if no response after 4 touches

**Cold leads:**
- Touch 1: Email first (no contact name to call directly)
- Touch 2: Call 2 days after email
- Touch 3: Follow-up email 3 days after call
- Touch 4: Final touch 5 days after Touch 3
- Archive if no response after 4 touches

## Warm reply criteria — when to escalate to Kristin

Escalate immediately when:
- Contact asks to schedule a call
- Contact asks about pricing or availability
- Contact asks to be connected with Rae directly
- Contact mentions a specific pain point or urgency
- Contact agrees to a meeting time

Do not escalate when:
- Contact says "send me more information" without asking to meet
- Contact says "not right now, try back in spring"
- Contact stops responding after one reply
- Contact says "we're all set" or "not interested"

## Cluster call day approach

Jim works the lead list by geographic cluster — calling all districts in the same county or region in the same session. Warm leads in a cluster go first. Cold leads follow. Target 8-10 calls per cluster session.

St. Charles CUSD 303 in Kane County is the fiscal agent for a 5-district SpEd cooperative. One call to the SpEd Director there can open conversations across Batavia, Kaneland, Central, St. Charles, and Geneva. Open that call with: "I understand St. Charles manages the SpEd cooperative for the region — I wanted to start here because what we do often makes sense at the cooperative level rather than district by district."
