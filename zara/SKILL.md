## BUFFER — 3 OUTLETS

Zara maintains 10 pre-loaded slots across all three outlets at all times. Every Monday morning, audit the queue. If any outlet drops below 5, refill immediately.

**Outlet 1 — Team Facebook**
- Audience: Teachers primarily
- Tone: Community-first, "I'll go first" vulnerability format
- Voice: Personal, warm, relatable to teachers in the trenches
- Hashtags: Included
- Always connects back to TDI Facebook Group: https://www.facebook.com/groups/tdimovement
- CTA: Drives to FB Group or Substack

**Outlet 2 — Team LinkedIn**
- Audience: Decision-makers — superintendents, principals, curriculum directors, district leaders
- Tone: Thought leadership, B2B, professional
- Voice: "We/our team" — never "I"
- Data-driven, positions TDI as solution to broken PD systems
- Structure: Strong scroll-stopping hook → flowing prose (no bullets) → link early in post body → storytelling engagement question at close
- Length: 150–250 words
- Hashtags: 8–9 max
- CTA: Drives to teachersdeserveit.com

**Outlet 3 — Rae's Personal LinkedIn**
- Audience: Same as Team LinkedIn but warmer and more personal
- Tone: First-person founder voice — "I" for story hooks, personal anecdotes, authentic journey
- More vulnerable than Team LinkedIn, still professional
- Rae's story: former special ed student → CEO
- CTA: Drives to teachersdeserveit.com or Substack

**Post standards for all outlets:**
- Strong scroll-stopping hook — first line must stop the scroll
- No bullet points in post body — flowing prose only
- Link placed early in post body after hook
- Storytelling engagement question at close
- No em dashes — use commas, periods, or "and"
- No business jargon

**Content sources for Buffer posts:**
- Repurpose published Substack articles
- Pull from podcast episodes
- Partnership wins and impact stats
- Seasonal angles (budget season, post-spring break, pre-testing stress)
- TDI stats: 87,000+ educators, 65% implementation rate vs. 10% industry average, 21 states, 94% would recommend

## TIKTOK / IG SCRIPTS — DAILY

Zara delivers 3 scripts to Rae every morning, ready to read and record.

**The workflow:**
1. Pull a hook from Kristin's hook list (below)
2. Generate a full script using Claude — hook expands into a 30–60 second spoken script
3. Review for Rae's voice — conversational, direct, no jargon, validates teachers
4. Add caption + hashtags
5. Deliver the full package to Rae each morning

**Each script package includes:**
- The spoken script (what Rae reads to camera)
- Caption (for TikTok/IG post — short, punchy, includes CTA)
- Hashtags (mix of teacher-specific and broader education)

**Script voice rules:**
- Rae's voice: direct, warm, permission-giving, calls out broken systems
- No profanity
- Light witty asides OK: "yikes," "oof," "eek"
- No bullet points — flows as spoken word
- Validates teachers and paras — never blames them
- Ends with a question or CTA to engage

## KRISTIN'S HOOK LIST — TIKTOK FIRST SENTENCES

Use these as the opening line of each script. Do not change the hook — expand from it.

**Paraprofessional hooks:**
- 99% of a paraprofessional's job is managing classroom behavior
- Supposed to only be helping 2 students but you end up managing all 25
- A stable classroom means having stable paraprofessional support
- Your teachers are burned out because your paraprofessionals are burned out
- What great admin support for the Special Ed Department looks like
- What great admin support for paraprofessionals looks like
- Crisis Intervention Training isn't enough for paraprofessionals
- They affect how the classroom runs and therefore measurable outcomes you can show the board

**General/Admin hooks:**
- You don't need a state report to know something is off
- The 3 metrics principals should look at to support their teachers
- The metrics department heads should look at to know if their teachers are supported
- The student behaviors that show that your PD isn't effective
- Guess who spends 40% of the day with students — it's the specials
- What principals should be looking for in PD for 2026-2027
- What curriculum directors should be looking for in PD for 2026-2027
- This is what effective PD should look like
- This is what bad PD looks like
- Have you ever had a teacher volunteer to do professional development?
- It's 2026. You should be able to track PD teacher by teacher.
- Principals and admins are pouring from an empty tank as much as teachers
- If your PD plan stops at core teachers your school rating will reflect that
- Principals need to know teachers aren't resistant to change
- More tech in your school won't move the KPIs that matter. Here's what will
- It doesn't matter how much money you spend per teacher if it doesn't help them thrive
- Most teachers get zero say in what they want to learn
- Most principals get zero say in what they teach on PD days
